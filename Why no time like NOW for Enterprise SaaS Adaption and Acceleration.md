The world, especially the Enterprise world, is now at an inflection point. The covid-19 situation has put the operational resiliency of enterprises to test, definitely on a short term; and simultaneously has forced them to view their mid-term and long-term evolution through a different lens.

In this backdrop is the convergence of three factors driving the forces of businesses. The first one over a decade in making, while the other two becoming relevant for a while and have magnified due to the unique circumstances we are subject to in pandemic conditions.

### **Three converging factors**

Firstly, we see the industries around the world increasingly grappling with complex and sophisticated realities to meet their customer's evolving expectations on their experience interacting with the brands. The consumer world has been constantly re-imagining customer experience, and the lead time for replicating similar experience in businesses is exponentially shrinking at a scorching pace. At the same time, enterprises are juggling with varied priorities to keep their costs right, increase their operational efficiency, and to stay relevant in the market they operate in. Be it Retailers looking to improve their consumer advocacy in-store and out of store; or Manufacturer seeking for real-time visibility and elasticity of its supply chain; or a bank looking to expand its bottom line while complying with the regulations set by various geographies they operate in; the business needs are becoming increasingly specific to the industry they exist, specific to the markets they operate in, and specific to the problems they solve, while seeking to stay relevant to its customers and aiming to generate returns to its shareholders.

Secondly, the evolving pandemic situation has fundamentally altered the way businesses perceive time. No longer is a 2 years or 3 years road-map merely sufficient in any realistic digital transformation strategy. With the aversion of risks assuming center-stage to strategic deliberations, businesses not only have to punctuate their transformation road-maps with measurable milestones, but also have to execute business objectives rapidly without compromising the scale, stability, security and effectiveness of the program.

Thirdly, enterprises' ability to expand or shrink on cue is no longer just a competitive capability, but has become a key for survival. The need to execute multiple experiments in the market, all the same time, and to simultaneously evaluate and respond to the outcome, and alter the course of action if need be, with different yardsticks applied to reflect the uniqueness of each initiative is paramount to thrive in the industry. This ability to be sentient to external forces is demonstrably pertinent in a world demanding real-time strategic interventions.

![](images/cover.png)

### **NOW is the time**

The 3 converging factors necessitates enterprises to deploy solutions which accommodate increasingly sophisticated and complex business needs in the industry they operate in; that enable change at speed, and provides the ability to scale-up or down on demand. Any digital transformations executed with such solutions as the technology foundation are destined to achieve its objectives with minimal strain to its resources. Enterprise SaaS solutions are by no means new to the market, however have become increasingly contextual to wide-ranging business scenarios over years, and have diverged into multiple sub-industries. With cloud becoming ubiquitous, and with the enterprise architecture attuned for integration across systems, SaaS solutions find themselves in an ecosystem where the surround solutions do not offer any constraints to operate alongside them. With the widening reach of SaaS solutions, it is now easier to build or acquire expertise to fuel digital transformation journey.

It is in this underlying circumstance of Enterprise SaaS solutions capable of addressing the sophisticated needs of various industries, and the culmination of factors that led enterprises irrevocably alter its view on the pace of change and scale of change, that has poised Enterprise SaaS solutions as the catalyst for success of Digital Transformation initiatives.
